Healthy Vision

Crisp detail and accurate color representation. No obstruction in the crystalline lens. Full contrast and sharp focal clarity — what healthy eyes see every day.

Early Stage

Mild clouding begins as protein aggregates form. Images appear slightly dull or dim. Often unnoticed by the patient. This is the optimal window for intervention.

Immature Cataract

Significant yellowing and distortion. Contrast is heavily reduced. Reading and driving become difficult. Protein aggregation increases intra-lenticular light scattering.

Mature Cataract

Severe obstruction. Vision is reduced to a murky, solid blur. Dense nuclear opacity dominates. Surgical intervention — lens replacement with IOL — is the only treatment.

How to Read the Result
Cataract Present
Clear imaging evidence of cataract detected. Lens opacity patterns are consistent with clinically significant cataract.
→ Clinical evaluation by an eye specialist is recommended.
Likely Early (Immature) Cataract
Imaging patterns suggest early or immature cataract formation. Structural lens changes are present but not advanced.
→ Routine monitoring or clinical correlation is advised.
Likely Non-Cataract (Early Overlap)
No definitive cataract detected. Subtle lens patterns may overlap with very early features or normal physiological variation.
→ No immediate action required. Monitor if symptoms develop.
No Cataract Detected
Lens appearance is consistent with a healthy lens. No imaging evidence of cataract is observed.
→ No follow-up required at this time.
